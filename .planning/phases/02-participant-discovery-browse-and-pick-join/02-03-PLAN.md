## Phase Goal (user story)

**As a** participant who picked a specific open cohort, **I want to** be told deterministically whether I got seated or the cohort just filled or closed, **so that** I never sit watching a dead spinner and I can pick another cohort when mine is gone. (Derived from the Phase 2 ROADMAP goal + PART-02 + criterion 3.)

<objective>
Rewire the browser participant store's join lifecycle for browse-and-pick (PART-02, D-14) and make the seated/full/closed outcome deterministic (D-06/D-12). Thread the picked `cohortId` through `join(baseUrl, cohortId)` into `createParticipant`, treat `cohort-ready` (not `cohort-joined`) as the definitive seat, remove the vague `JOIN_WATCHDOG_MS` no-advert timer, and replace it with a directory-driven outcome: while awaiting a seat, a bounded directory poll transitions to a distinct "that cohort just filled or closed" state when the picked cohort leaves the Advertised set, while a directory fetch error is ignored so unreachable never reads as closed.

Purpose: the store is the single lifecycle owner for the browse flow (plan 04 wires the UI onto it). This delivers the error-state/validation refinement of the join capability proven headlessly in plan 01 - deterministic, no dead spinners - without any new backend or protocol.

Output: `join(baseUrl, cohortId)`, the `seated`/`joinClosed` state + the exported `pickedCohortClosed` helper + the `handleDirectorySnapshot` transition, the `JOIN_WATCHDOG_MS`/`joinWatchdog` removal, and an extended `participant.spec.ts`.
</objective>
