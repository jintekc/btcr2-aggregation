## Phase Goal (user story)

**As a** participant pointed at a service's URL, **I want to** land on that service's directory and browse its open cohorts with enough detail to choose one, **so that** I can tell which cohorts are open, how full they are, and how big the co-sign group is before I commit. (Derived from the Phase 2 ROADMAP goal + PART-01.)

<objective>
Turn the participant landing at `/` into the discover-first browse surface (PART-01, criterion 1). Add a neutral `lib/directory.ts` (the public reads + the pure joinability/label helpers), a service-identity header and a ~5s-polled directory list built entirely from the existing `DirectoryCohortDTO` and the Phase-1 in-house primitives, and restructure `App.tsx` so `BrowseView` is the front door (D-13) in place of the old KeyGen-first entry.

Purpose: a stranger can navigate to the service origin and read a live, plain-language list of the open cohorts - beacon type, network, seats, co-sign threshold, status, cohort id - enough to choose one, with distinct empty and unreachable states, and nothing but anonymous public reads. The pick/join interaction is added in plan 04 on top of this surface.

Output: `packages/web/src/lib/directory.ts` (+ spec), the `browse/{ServiceIdentityHeader,DirectoryList,CohortRow,BrowseView}.tsx` components (+ a DirectoryList spec), and the `App.tsx` landing restructure.
</objective>
